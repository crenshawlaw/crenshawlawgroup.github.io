Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about business law, collections, and contract enforcement.

These FAQs provide general information for businesses facing contract disputes, unpaid accounts, royalty issues, compliance concerns, and commercial risk.

Contract Enforcement

What is contract enforcement?

Contract enforcement involves taking steps to make sure a party honors the terms of an agreement. This may include reviewing the contract, identifying the breach, sending a demand letter, negotiating resolution, or pursuing legal remedies.

What should a business do when a contract is breached?

A business should preserve documents, review the agreement, identify missed obligations, calculate damages, and seek legal guidance before escalating the dispute.

Can a contract dispute be resolved without litigation?

Yes. Many contract disputes can be resolved through demand letters, negotiation, settlement discussions, or revised performance terms before litigation becomes necessary.

Demand Letters

What is the purpose of a demand letter?

A demand letter formally explains the issue, identifies the requested action, and gives the other party an opportunity to resolve the matter before further legal steps are taken.

When should a business send a demand letter?

A demand letter may be appropriate when invoices remain unpaid, contract terms are ignored, services are not performed, or a party refuses to correct a business dispute.

Collections & Account Recovery

When should a business pursue collections?

Businesses should consider collection action when invoices remain unpaid beyond agreed terms, communication breaks down, or the debtor repeatedly delays payment.

Can businesses recover unpaid invoices legally?

Yes. Businesses can pursue unpaid invoices through demand notices, negotiation, structured collection efforts, settlement agreements, or legal action depending on the facts.

What information is helpful for account recovery?

Helpful documents include contracts, invoices, payment history, emails, purchase orders, delivery records, account statements, and any written acknowledgment of the debt.

Royalty Collections & License Enforcement

What are royalty collections?

Royalty collections involve recovering payments owed under licensing, revenue-sharing, intellectual property, or commercial agreements.

What if royalty reports appear inaccurate?

A business may need to review the license agreement, request supporting records, analyze payment calculations, and pursue enforcement if underpayment is identified.

Can license agreements be enforced after repeated nonpayment?

Yes. Depending on the agreement, a business may be able to demand payment, terminate rights, pursue damages, or enforce other contractual remedies.

Compliance & Risk Management

What is risk management in business law?

Risk management means identifying legal and contractual exposure before it becomes a larger dispute. This can involve reviewing agreements, clarifying duties, and strengthening internal processes.

How can a business reduce contract disputes?

Businesses can reduce disputes by using clear written agreements, documenting communications, setting payment deadlines, defining remedies, and reviewing obligations before problems arise.

Business Disputes

What types of disputes fall under business law?

Business law disputes may involve contracts, payments, licensing, vendors, service providers, partners, customers, compliance obligations, or commercial relationships.

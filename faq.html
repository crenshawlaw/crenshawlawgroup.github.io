Frequently Asked Questions

Business Law, Enforcement & Collections

Contract Enforcement

A breach occurs when a party fails to perform obligations required under a valid agreement without lawful excuse.

In many cases, a demand letter strengthens your position and can resolve the issue without litigation.

Time limits depend on jurisdiction and contract type. Prompt legal review is recommended.

Collections & Recovery

When internal collection efforts fail and invoices remain unpaid beyond agreed terms.

If the contract permits interest or late fees, they may be recoverable.

Disputed invoices require contract review and structured enforcement strategy.

Royalty & Licensing Disputes

Improper reporting, delayed payments, and unauthorized deductions are common concerns.

Many licensing disputes can be resolved through negotiation or structured enforcement letters.

Risk & Compliance

Reviewing contractual exposure and compliance obligations helps prevent disputes before they arise.

Representation depends on jurisdiction and matter type. Contact the firm to discuss your situation.
